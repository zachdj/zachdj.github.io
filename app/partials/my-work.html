Auburn University REU - See and Avoid for Smart UAVs

I worked with Andy Morgan at the Summer 2016 REU with Smart UAVs at Auburn University. We developed a See-and-Avoid system for autonomous UAVs using C/C++. The system is divided into two components. The first is a test bed consisting of a simulation of a front-facing camera. The simulation is built with OpenGL. The second component is a visible-light based collision detection and avoidance system. Objects in the FOV of the UAV are detected using a vision algorithm developed with OpenCV. If an object is determined to be a threat, an avoidance maneuver will be attempted.

You can follow our progress on the Bitbucket repository. You can also read the paper describing our research. We also post test videos and flight simulations on this Youtube channel.

Recent Projects

Chemical Concentration Simulator

The DOW Chemical Company and NASA Undergraduate Research grant funded a research project to build a simple simulation capable of providing business and political leaders quick and understandable information about the threat posed by a chemical spill. The application has since been taken over by Mark Adkins and awaits future improvements.

You can follow the evolution of the project here. The application itself is live and publicly available. It's hosted on a free platform and thus may take a few seconds to spin up.

SCRATCH Learning Tool

A local group of volunteers educate underprivileged children on sustainable gardening. I was lead developer on a year-long project to develop a suite of tools that would allow educators to assess the learning progress of low-literacy children using fun, web-based games.

We developed a RESTful Web API in ASP.NET as well as a front-end web interface for teachers and students using AngularJS. The games were written with the Phaser HTML5 game framework.

Monte Carlo Tennis Predictor

As part of the MTH411 - Mathematical Modeling course at Marshall University, I developed a piece of software that attempts to predict the outcome of tennis matches using a Monte Carlo simulation with a Markov Chain.

For more information, see the GitHub repository.
